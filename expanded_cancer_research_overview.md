# Enhanced Cancer Types and Causes Research

## 1. Classification of Cancer Types
Cancers are broadly classified into the following main types:
- **Carcinoma:** Originates in epithelial tissues, accounting for 80-90% of all cancers.
- **Sarcoma:** Develops in supportive and connective tissues.
- **Myeloma:** Arises from plasma cells.
- **Leukemia:** Affects blood and bone marrow.
- **Lymphoma:** Originates in lymphatic tissues.
- **Others:** Including germ cell tumors, mesothelioma, etc.

This classification is supported by authoritative sources, including the National Cancer Institute and SEER.

## 2. Epidemiological Data
- **Global Incidence:** In 2020, approximately 18.1 million new cases were diagnosed worldwide.
- **Projections:** Estimated to reach 28 million new cases annually by 2040.
- **United States Data (2026 Estimates):** Over 2.1 million new cases, with the most common types being:
  - Men: Prostate, lung, colorectal.
  - Women: Breast, lung, colorectal.
- **Risk Factors:** Smoking, alcohol, poor diet, infections, demographic disparities.

Sources include CDC, Cancer Research UK, and the American Cancer Society.

## 3. Mapping to Medical Ontologies
Cancer types are mapped to standardized vocabularies such as:
- **SNOMED CT:** Provides comprehensive clinical terminology.
- **ICD-10:** Used for disease classification and billing.
- **UMLS:** Facilitates mapping among various biomedical vocabularies.

These mappings support interoperability and are maintained by organizations like SNOMED International.
