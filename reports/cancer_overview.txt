### Cancer Types, Causes, Symptoms, and Diagnosis Methods

#### Cancer Types
Cancer is not a single disease but a group of related diseases characterized by uncontrolled cell growth. Some of the most common types include:

- **Breast Cancer:** Originates in the cells of the breast tissue, often presenting as a lump or mass. It is more common in women but can also affect men.
- **Lung Cancer:** Arises in the lung tissues, frequently linked to smoking. Symptoms often include persistent cough and breathlessness.
- **Prostate Cancer:** Common in men, developing in the prostate gland, often detected through screening tests like PSA.
- **Colorectal Cancer:** Affects the colon or rectum, with symptoms such as changes in bowel habits or blood in stool.
- **Skin Cancer:** Includes melanoma and non-melanoma types, often caused by UV exposure, presenting as unusual moles or skin lesions.

Each cancer type has unique features and behaviors but shares common pathways that can be targeted for treatment.

---

#### Causes of Cancer
Cancer develops when genetic mutations occur in cells, disrupting normal growth controls. Causes include:

- **Genetic Factors:** Inherited mutations can predispose individuals to certain cancers.
- **Environmental Exposures:** Tobacco smoke, radiation, and certain chemicals increase risk.
- **Lifestyle Factors:** Unhealthy diets, lack of physical activity, and excessive alcohol consumption are linked to higher risk.
- **Infections:** Some viruses and bacteria, such as HPV and H. pylori, can cause cancer.
- **Age:** The risk generally increases with age due to accumulated genetic damage.

While some risk factors are modifiable, others are inherent or unavoidable.

---

#### Symptoms of Cancer
Symptoms vary depending on the type and location but often include:

- Unexplained weight loss
- Persistent fatigue
- Pain that does not resolve
- Changes in skin appearance or sores that do not heal
- Unusual bleeding or discharge
- Lumps or thickening in body tissues
- Changes in bowel or bladder habits

Early detection of symptoms is critical for effective treatment.

---

#### Diagnosis Methods
Detecting cancer early improves outcomes significantly. Common diagnostic methods include:

- **Imaging Tests:** X-rays, CT scans, MRI, PET scans help locate tumors.
- **Biopsies:** Removal of tissue samples for microscopic examination remains the definitive diagnosis.
- **Blood Tests:** Tumor markers or other blood indicators can suggest the presence of cancer.
- **Endoscopy:** Uses a flexible tube with a camera to examine internal organs.
- **Genetic Testing:** Identifies inherited mutations or specific cancer markers.

Advances in diagnostic technology, including molecular and genetic profiling, are increasingly enabling personalized treatment strategies.
